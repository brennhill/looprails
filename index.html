Danger zone

Review is a trap → prevent, don't review

When stakes are high but a human can't reliably catch the error in time, a confirmation gate just creates a rubber stamp and a scapegoat. Don't review — prevent: make it reversible, shrink the blast radius, sandbox it, force a real decision, or forbid the action and escalate.

The method

Four moves: Grade · Guard · Show · Prove.

Per action, not per system. Natural standup questions: did you grade it? how's it guarded? what do we show the human? have we proven it works?

MOVE 1

Grade

Score every action by blast radius × reversibility × stakes → G0–G3.

MOVE 2

Guard

Match control to grade. Where a human can't catch it, prevent — don't review.

MOVE 3

Show

Design the moment: clarity, consequences, provenance, detection — within an attention budget.

MOVE 4

Prove

Measure whether humans actually catch errors. Red-team the oversight, not just the agent.

The guarantees

Every governed action stays on the RAIL.

Four rails keep an agent on track — the properties a well-governed action always keeps.

R

Reversible

Undo by default, or the blast radius is contained. Reserve hard gates for the truly irreversible.

A

Authorized

Permission scaled to the grade. Least privilege; the proposer isn't the approver for high-stakes acts.

I

Interruptible

A real override — anyone can pull the andon cord; a kill switch stops everything without diagnosing.

L

Logged

An observable, provable trail — so you can validate the oversight actually works, not just exists.
